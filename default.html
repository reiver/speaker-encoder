Speaker Encoder

voice → (speaker encoder) → speaker embeddin

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

A speaker encoder is a function that takes a (usually human) voice as input, and outputs a vector — called the speaker embedding — that characterizes or identifies the speaker. In general, this speaker embedding is expected to be unique to each speaker.

A speaker embedding tends to contain information about the speaker's pitch, texture, tone, etc.

Usage

Some things that speaker encoders are used for includes:

Speaker Diarization
speech separation
speaker verification
trying to determine whether two voice samples are from the same speaker.
voice cloning & TTS
mimicking a specific person's voice using a sample of only a few seconds of that specific person's voice.
