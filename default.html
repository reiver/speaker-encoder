Speaker Encoder

voice → (speaker encoder) → speaker embeddin

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

A speaker encoder is a technique in speaker recognition.

A speaker encoder is a function that takes a (usually human) voice as input, and outputs a vector — called the speaker embedding — that characterizes or identifies the speaker. In general, this speaker embedding is expected to be unique'ish for each speaker.

More precisely, a speaker encoder maps variable-length speech into a fixed-length vector where the speaker embeddings from voice samples from the same speaker are close together and different speakers are far apart.

A speaker embedding tends to contain information about the speaker's pitch, texture, tone, etc.

Big Picture

THe big picture is:

The objective of the training is not to produce a "correct" speaker ID directly (like in many instance of supervised learning), but instead the objective is to organize the embedding space such that:

  • speech samples from the same speaker cluster together
  • speech samples from different speakers are well separated

Usage

Some things that speaker encoders are used for includes:

speaker diarization
speech separation
speaker verification
trying to determine whether two voice samples are from the same speaker.
voice cloning & TTS
mimicking a specific person's voice using a sample of only a few seconds of that specific person's voice.
